Research

Two things I'm building because they should exist, and mostly don't.

MSME Pulse

Seeing small-business stress before it becomes failure.

India has 63 million small businesses. They employ 110 million people and generate close to a third of the country's output — and when one of them slides toward collapse, almost nobody sees it coming. The early-warning systems that watch over large firms were never built for a workshop with a dozen employees.

MSME Pulse is built for exactly them. It reads signals that move before a balance sheet does — factory activity from NASA's nighttime-lights data, sentiment drawn from Hindi and English financial news, and regional economic indicators — and turns them into a live map of where small-business stress is building, by region and sector. One of the first early-warning systems aimed at this segment rather than at the companies big enough to already have one. The bet is simple: catch the strain forming weeks before it becomes a default, while there's still time to do something about it.

TrustGraph

Making 400 million invisible people scorable.

Roughly 400 million adults in India have no credit score. Not because they're risky — because they've never had a formal loan or a credit card, and so they've never generated the paper trail a bank needs to say yes. Invisibility gets treated as risk, and they get locked out.

TrustGraph scores them anyway. In place of a credit history they don't have, it reads alternative signals and returns a score a lender can act on — and, just as important, one it can explain. Every decision comes with the reasons behind it and clears a fairness check, so the model has to be accountable, not only accurate. The point isn't a cleverer algorithm. It's that hundreds of millions of people could move from unscorable to scorable, on terms defensible to a regulator and fair to the borrower.
